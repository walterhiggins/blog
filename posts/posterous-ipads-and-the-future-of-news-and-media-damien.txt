# iPads and the future of news and media «  Damien Mulley
## 2010/04/25 04:38:14 

<div class="posterous_bookmarklet_entry">
  <blockquote class="posterous_long_quote"><p>It hasn't got multitasking, a camera, USB ports, is certainly not open yet is still vastly superior to anything out there. There are already amazing applications on it. Yet bringing in a new paperboy to deliver the newspaper to the door isn't going to save you folks, is it? </p>
<p><a href="http://berglondon.com/blog/2010/04/02/popularscienceplus/">Popular Science</a>, <a href="http://www.coolhunting.com/tech/cool-hunting-ip.php">Coolhunting</a> and that <a href="http://technomarketer.typepad.com/technomarketer/2010/04/technology-redefines-categories-and-experience.html">Alice in Wonderland</a> interactive book have been lauded as great so far for iPad but they're on a device that's a fraction of one percent of the people that daily consume media. If you wait for all of them to go all iPad buying then you'll be very broke. Innovate what you're doing, not how you deliver.</p></blockquote>
