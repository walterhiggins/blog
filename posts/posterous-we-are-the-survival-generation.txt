<p>&ldquo;We are the survival generation,&rdquo; said Ms. Higaki, who works part time at a furniture store.</p>
<p>Hisakazu Matsuda, president of Japan Consumer Marketing Research Institute, who has written several books on Japanese consumers, has a different name for Japanese in their 20s; he calls them the consumption-haters. He estimates that by the time this generation hits their 60s, their  habits of frugality will have cost the Japanese economy $420 billion in lost consumption.</p>
