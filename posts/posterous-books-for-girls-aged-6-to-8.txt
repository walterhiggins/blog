# Books for girls aged 6 to 8
## 2010/02/13 03:36:00 

<p>This is a list of books my daughter has read herself or which have been read to her. I'm listing them here so others might find this list useful. (Disclaimer - the links are to Amazon.com and yes I get a tiny cut if the book is purchased). In no particular order...</p>
<ol>
<li><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0670062766?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0670062766">Pippi Longstocking</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0670062766" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" /> This has been a longtime favourite. It's a collection of short stories about an adventurous girl - some of them very funny for adults too. Has been read and re-read many times. </li>
<li><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0763631612?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0763631612">The Secret Garden</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0763631612" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" /> Can't say much about this as I haven't read it but our daughter loved it (see more Frances Hodgson Burnett on the list). </li>
<li><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0142413828?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0142413828">Esio Trot</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0142413828" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" /> Again - can't say much about this other than you can't go far wrong with Roald Dahl.</li>
<li><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/006029809X?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=006029809X">The Trouble Begins: A Box of Unfortunate Events, Books 1-3 (The Bad Beginning; The Reptile Room; The Wide Window)</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=006029809X" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" /> Fun up to a point but these books do get a little repetitive. We read up to book 4 - it's pretty much the same book as the first 3. Personally I think the film adaptation is better than the books (but that's just me). </li>
<li><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0060581867?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0060581867">Little Town on the Prairie (Little House)</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0060581867" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" /> Now we're talking. My daughter has been spared the horror that was the 70's television show. Don't let the TV show color your perception - these are wonderful children's classics (in a very similar vein to Anne of the green gables). Also recommended are <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0060581832?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0060581832">On the Banks of Plum Creek (Little House)</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0060581832" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" />, <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0061289809?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0061289809">Little House in the Big Woods</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0061289809" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" />, and if you can get your hands on the full color edition of <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0060581840?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0060581840">By the Shores of Silver Lake (Little House)</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0060581840" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" /> do.</li>
<li><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0156012197?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0156012197">The Little Prince</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0156012197" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" /> Very French and very philosophical but still a good read. It's a slim book with many illustrations by the author.</li>
<li><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0763625299?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0763625299">The Tale of Despereaux: Being the Story of a Mouse, a Princess, Some Soup and a Spool of Thread</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0763625299" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" /> Our daughter had seen the movie adaptation before reading this. It's a little dark in places but still a good read.</li>
<li><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1420925296?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1420925296">A Little Princess</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=1420925296" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" /> Another children's classic from Frances Hodgson Burnett about a little rich girl who falls on hard times but whose pluck and basic goodness shines through.</li>
<li><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0553609416?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0553609416">The Complete Anne of Green Gables Boxed Set (Anne of Green Gables, Anne of Avonlea, Anne of the Island, Anne of Windy Poplars, Anne's House of Dreams, ... Rainbow Valley, Rilla of Ingleside)</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0553609416" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" /> I can't recommend these books enough. They are now more than 100 years old but to children they're timeless. Chronicling the childhood and early adulthood of Anne Shirley - a girl who lives on Prince Edward Island, Canada - we've read 'Anne of Green Gables', 'Anne of Avonlea', 'Anne of the Island' (and another whose title escapes me). </li>
<li><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/014132256X?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=014132256X">Heidi (Puffin Classics)</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=014132256X" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" /> Another classic. I haven't read this one (my wife read it) but any of the Puffin Classics are a safe bet.</li>
<li><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0603560741?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0603560741">The Wishing Chair Collections: Three Exciting Stories in One</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0603560741" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" /> Enid Blyton's "Wishing Chair" trilogy is a great read - one of our daughter's favorites.</li>
<li><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1405201711?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=xanajavaperle-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1405201711">The Faraway Tree Stories: Three Books in One</a><img src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=xanajavaperle-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=1405201711" border="0" height="1" alt="" width="1" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" /> Also by Enid Blyton.</li>
</ol>
